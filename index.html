Everything you need to know about the Alabama Cost Curve Summit

The Summit

Why this event exists

Healthcare costs in Alabama continue to rise — but they don't have to. The status quo benefits intermediaries, not patients or the employers who pay for their care. The Alabama Cost Curve Summit was created to change that dynamic.

We bring healthcare providers and employer groups to the same table, united around a shared goal: making healthcare more transparent, more competitive, and more affordable for everyone.

Through keynote presentations and open discussion, attendees hear from thought leaders and practitioners who are already bending the trend in their own organizations — real stories, real data, real results.

This isn't a vendor showcase. It's a working session for people who are serious about fixing a broken system from the inside.

Who Should Come

Built for decision-makers

The summit is designed for approximately 75 carefully curated attendees — because quality of conversation matters more than quantity of seats. If you influence how healthcare is purchased, delivered, or managed in Alabama, this event is for you.

Presenting at the Summit
Keynote Speaker
Matt Ohrt
Founder · DFTB Consulting & Self Fund Health
Over a 25-year HR career, Matt served as an executive leader for Toyota, Fortune Brands, and Badger Mining — the #1 Great Place to Work in America. As VP of HR at Merrill Steel, he led a five-year health plan transformation that saved the company and employees over M.
Matt founded the Healthcare Best Practice Group (20,000+ members), co-founded Self Fund Health — the fastest-growing employer health plan in the Midwest — and authored Save Your Company, Don’t Feed the Beast (6,500+ copies sold). In 2025, he became National Executive Director of the Free Market Medical Association.
📍 Employer Health Plan Transformation & Free Market Healthcare
Session Speaker
Patrick Moore
CEO · Pretekt
“Healthcare is a Racket — Call in the Marines”
With over 20 years of experience in the insurance and employee benefits industry, Patrick helps employers and advisors navigate the complex and evolving healthcare landscape. As CEO of Pretekt, his mission is to simplify healthcare for employers and employees alike — with a laser focus on the most consequential topic in the room: Compliance.
Pat is also a Master Sergeant in the Marine Corps Reserves, serving for 22 years before retiring in 2023. He brings that same mission-clarity to his belief that fiduciary responsibility will completely change the healthcare industry.
📍 Employer Fiduciary Responsibility & ERISA Compliance
Session Speaker
Kat Cagle
Wellness & Internal Communications Director · Phifer Incorporated
Kat Cagle is the Wellness Director and Internal Communications Director at Phifer Incorporated, where she supports both employee well-being and company-wide communication. She leads the PhiferCares Wellness Program, manages the PhiferCares Park facility, works closely with Phifer Healthcare and provider partners, and promotes engagement with onsite clinic services to help employees and their families thrive.
Kat also oversees internal communication strategies — including company-wide digital signage and the employee mobile app — to ensure timely, clear, and accessible information across the organization.
📍 Turning the Tide on Healthcare Costs: A Real-World Success Story
Panel Host
Spencer Smith
SVP · ParetoHealth  ·  Host, The Self-Funded Podcast
Spencer Smith is a healthcare industry expert and podcast host known for his work on the Self-Funded Podcast, where he discusses health insurance practices, self-funding strategies, and industry trends. As SVP at ParetoHealth, he focuses on innovative healthcare solutions for employers.
His content blends educational discussions, interviews with healthcare professionals, and promotional material for industry events. He frequently covers topics like vertical integration in healthcare, employer health plans, and emerging industry regulations.
🎤 Moderating the Panel Session
Panelist
Kimberly Carleson
CEO · US Beacon
A nationally respected healthcare executive and patient advocate with 15+ years serving self-funded health plans, state governments, and Fortune 500 companies in reimbursement integrity, regulatory compliance, and fiduciary governance.
As CEO of US Beacon, Kimberly leads one of the nation’s most established independent medical claims compliance and integrity review firms. She has served as a panelist for ERIC and the National Auditors Association, with her work referenced in federal policy discussions on healthcare oversight.
📍 Claims Integrity, Fiduciary Governance & ERISA Compliance
Panelist
Amanda Volner
VP of Sales, SFC® · Healthee
Amanda Volner, SFC®, brings over two decades of experience in sales leadership, strategic partnerships, and business development in healthcare and benefits. Before Healthee, she served as VP of Enterprise Partnerships at Medefy Health and held leadership roles at Integrated Prescription Management (now LucyRx) and PPOk, expanding specialty services and network growth across the industry.
Her expertise spans SaaS sales, pharmacy benefit management, and employee benefits. Originally from Oklahoma, she brings dynamic leadership to Healthee’s mission of simplifying healthcare benefits and empowering businesses and employees nationwide.
📍 Employee Education & Engagement and Care Navigation
Panelist
Adam Leggette
RVP of Sales, Southeast · Apta Health
📍 Cash Pricing, Direct Contracts & Price Transparency
Panelist
Hannah Denson
Market VP of Sales · VerusRx
Hannah serves as the Market Vice President of Sales for VerusRx, a PBM formed with the mission of helping employers manage prescription drug costs while improving health outcomes for their employees. In an industry that can be difficult to navigate, she is committed to helping employers curb rapidly rising healthcare costs while guiding members through the challenging and complex maze of healthcare.
Hannah serves as the President of Ladies Executive Golf Society (LEGS) of Syracuse, empowering women to embrace golf as a tool for networking, skill development, business expansion, and most importantly having fun. When she’s not battling healthcare costs or hitting the links, you’ll find her spending time with her husband and two dogs.
